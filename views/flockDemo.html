Swarm Intelligence: Borrowing From Nature

Simulating a force of nature, this demonstration emulates the driving force behind herds of animals and swarms of insects. Swarming intelligence has many different uses ranging from visual effects and problem solution optimization all the way to driving a collection of robots or video game characters.

Click the "Game Type" button to toggle between Wolves and Zombies. Clear will empty the current screen and start will restart the game from scratch.

Wolf mode will have the predators chasing down and eating the prey (sheep) until there are none left!
Zombie mode will have the predators shambling toward the humans and biting them, turning them into even more zombies until nothing but zombies remain!

Stats

  • Amount: Amount of Predators/Prey at the start of a game
  • Speed: The velocity at which the predator/prey travels
  • Cohesion: The "glue" force, or what draws the predators/prey toward one another
  • Separation: The repelling force, or what drives the predators/prey away from one another
  • Alignment: The force that keeps the predators/prey headed in the same direction
